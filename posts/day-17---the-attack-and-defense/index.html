Day 17 - The Attack and Defense

Dec 17, 2024

Introduction

Choosing between attack and defense can be challenging. To make the right decision, you need to focus on what’s urgent versus what’s big, and prioritize your own groups before targeting your opponent.

Urgent vs Big Move

Urgent Move

Urgent moves are about handling immediate threats. Even if the stakes seem small, urgent moves prevent disasters and stabilize your position.

In this example, we can see that the move in A8 (triangle) is an urgent move because it decides the life and death of the Black group.

Big Move

Big moves offer significant rewards but are often less urgent. Once your urgent issues are resolved, pursue big moves to gain an advantage.

In this diagram, the move in E3 (triangle) which is a komoku is a big move. This move can help Black to consolidate the corner into territory while it can help deny this territory if White plays here. This will not threaten in any case the Black stone that can jump in the opposite side to find a life for itself.

Defense vs Attack Move

Defense Move

Defensive moves strengthen your position and address vulnerabilities. Use them when you need to stabilize, recover, or protect your assets. They are not passive; a good defense involves actively ensuring your weaknesses are covered and your position is unassailable.

Attack Move

Attacking puts pressure on your opponent, forcing them to react. Well-timed attacks can disrupt their plans, weaken their position, and secure advantages for you. However, attacks must be strategic to avoid overextending yourself.

Priority: Four Questions to Help You Decide

When deciding between attack and defense, ask yourself these four questions:

  1. Do you have a weak group?

    • If yes, reinforce it. A weak group can be attacked easily and cause problems.

  2. Does your opponent have a weak group?

    • If yes, plan to attack it. Weak groups are easy to target and can give you an advantage.

  3. Can you make a big territorial move?

    • If yes, do it. Big moves can change the game and put you ahead.

  4. Could your opponent make a big territorial move, or do they have a big territory?

    • If yes, try to stop them. Prevent them from gaining more or reduce what they already have.

Proverb of the Day

“Strengthening your own weak group makes your opponent’s weaker.”

This principle emphasizes the importance of fortifying your position. When you reinforce your weak groups, you not only protect yourself but also deny your opponent easy opportunities to attack. In essence, shoring up your defenses indirectly weakens their strategy by limiting their viable options.
